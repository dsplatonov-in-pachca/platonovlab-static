Baking worksheet

R2-C1: Borodinsky Sourdough Bread

A public working timeline for the iPad: each item can be checked during the bake, with the facts that need to be captured for the next conclusion.

Full recipe

Technical summary

Course code
R2-C1 — advanced regional rye bread after the R1 fundamentals
Lesson theory
scald, red rye malt, coriander, sourdough acidity, honey sweetness, and 12–24 h crumb rest
Scald
malt + 100 ml boiling water, 3–12 hours at room temperature
Bulk ferment
8–10 hours at 22–24 °C, volume up 1.5–2x
Proof
1.5–2 hours, about 1.5 cm above the pan rim
Bake
250 °C for 10 minutes, then 200 °C for 75 minutes; crumb ≥ 98 °C
Sensory
acidity, sweetness, malt, coriander, moisture, knife stickiness, density, and aftertaste after resting

Next bake: confirm the formula and collect missing data

  1. Bring the starter to active condition before mixing; record refresh time and approximate kitchen temperature.

    Step ingredients

    Active 100% rye starter
    100 g in the dough
    Refreshment
    make at least 120 g total as reserve; record actual grams
    Target
    Active 100% hydration rye starter, 100 g used in the recipe.
    Check
    Bubbles, dome or beginning of recession, sour aroma without harsh vinegar.
    Evidence
    Time, kitchen temperature, photos of the jar from top and side.
    Photo
    Starter before mixing.

  2. Scald 30 g red rye malt with 100 ml boiling water and hold at room temperature.

    Step ingredients

    Red rye malt
    30 g
    Boiling water
    100 g
    Target
    Uniform dark paste with no dry pockets.
    Check
    Water is boiling; actual holding time is recorded.
    Evidence
    Timer, scald photo, kitchen temperature.
    Photo
    Scald before adding to dough.

  3. Combine flour, water, starter, scald, salt, honey, and coriander. Mix on speed 1–3 for 5 minutes.

    Step ingredients

    Whole-grain rye flour
    300 g
    Wheat flour
    100 g
    Active 100% rye starter
    100 g
    Scald
    all of it: 30 g malt + 100 g water
    Remaining water
    180 g
    Salt
    10 g
    Honey
    20 g
    Ground coriander
    5 g
    Target
    Sticky dough with a high proportion of rye flour and no dry areas.
    Check
    Record actual dough temperature after mixing.
    Evidence
    Mixer timer, dough temperature, texture photo.
    Photo
    Dough immediately after mixing.

  4. Ferment at 22–24 °C until volume increases about 1.5–2x.

    Step ingredients

    Mixed dough
    all of it
    New ingredients
    none
    Target
    Visible rise, surface without major collapse.
    Check
    If a cold pause is needed for scheduling, record before/after fridge photos as a separate experiment.
    Evidence
    Kitchen temperature, timer, before/after pause photos.
    Photo
    Dough before any pause and before shaping.

  5. Transfer dough to the pan, smooth the surface, proof for 1.5–2 hours.

    Step ingredients

    Fermented dough
    all of it
    Pan grease
    thin layer, not part of the formula
    Target
    Dough rises about 1.5 cm above the pan rim.
    Check
    Track height against the pan rim, not time alone.
    Evidence
    Side photo, proof timer, kitchen temperature.
    Photo
    Dough in the pan before baking.

  6. 250 °C for 10 minutes, then 200 °C for 75 minutes.

    Step ingredients

    Proofed dough
    all dough in the pan
    New ingredients
    none
    Target
    Internal crumb temperature at least 98 °C.
    Check
    Record actual centre temperature and oven-out time.
    Evidence
    Probe thermometer, timer, finished loaf photo.
    Photo

  7. Photograph the crumb at 12 and 24 hours, then evaluate stickiness and moisture.

    Step ingredients

    Finished loaf
    whole loaf after resting
    New ingredients
    none
    Target

    Check
    Do not use an early cut as the only source of evidence.
    Evidence
    Crumb photo, tasting note, note on knife stickiness.
