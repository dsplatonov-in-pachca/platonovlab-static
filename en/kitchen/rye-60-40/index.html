Baking worksheet

R1-C1: Rye-wheat bread 60/40

Run all times from the actual first refreshment. The key controls are starter readiness, temperature, pan rise, and crumb after a 12–24 h rest.

Full recipe

Technical summary

Course code
R1-C1 — first control lesson in the rye track
Lesson theory
rye acidity, pentosans, sticky dough, pan proofing, and 12–24 h crumb rest
Use case
default first choice when the goal is diagnosing dough behavior with a high proportion of rye flour before more complex formulas
Total formula
500 g flour: 300 g rye flour, 200 g wheat flour; 390 g water; 78% hydration; 10 g salt
Starter
150 g active rye starter at 100% hydration; run refreshments from the actual start and only after the previous stage is ready
170 g starter variant
if using the full 20+75+75 build, mix with 170 g starter, 215 g rye flour, 200 g wheat flour, 305 g water, and 10 g salt
Mix
after the final starter peak: starter + water + salt + optional yeast, then rye and wheat flours; dissolve coarse salt first
Mixing method
3–4 min with a Danish whisk or spatula to full homogeneity; no windowpane test and no extra flour because of stickiness
Pan
grease, load dough, smooth with a wet spoon, mark height
Proof
24–28 °C to 1.6–1.8x; 1.5–3 h is only an estimate, decide by dough state
Bake
after proof: 250 °C 10 min steam → 200 °C 25 min → 170 °C to 98 °C internal
Slice
wait at least 12 h, ideally 24 h; rye crumb needs time to stabilize
Sensory
acidity, rye aroma, moisture, knife stickiness, density, crumbliness, and aftertaste
